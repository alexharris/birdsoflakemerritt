Birds of Lake Merritt

a book about a lake and the birds at the lake

Lake Merritt is home to the nation's first official wildlife refuge, founded in 1869 on the edges of the then-small town of Oakland, California. In the intervening years, as Oakland has grown to fully encompass the water's edge, Lake Merritt has provided respite for birds and humans amongst it's waters and grassy circumfrence.

Birds of Lake Merritt is a book that tells the history of this lake, the relationship between the birds and humans that share it, and introduces us to fifteen species that can be regularly seen on or around it's waters.

Visiting the lake

Reading about the lake is great, but visiting is even better.

The rotary nature center is a great place to start a visit. The pond and nearby bird islands often have many types of birds visible, no binoculars needed. Walk in either direction and see what other kinds of birds you can spot.

Additionally, the Golden Gate Audubon Society hosts regular bird watching field trips at the lake, in case you want something with a little more structure.
